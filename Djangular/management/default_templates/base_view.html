I've been working with AngularJS and Django for over 4 years. Find more help and information here about using these two powerful frameworks together here. This website is powered by AngularJS and Django running on a Docker machine and a EC2 micro instance from Amazon AWS.

To kick things off in this new blog, I'd like to show you a new experimental technology I've begun gradually building by accident in the last 3 weeks. This new experimental technology is what I'd like to call Djangular. With this project, I hope to encourage new developers to try building websites made for a modern internet experience. This site will also show you how to build massive AngularJS apps that'll scale up to large projects without the fear of ending up with a slow and bloated behemoth.

With Djangular, most of the content you see here on this page is served up from PostgreSQL. HTML, js, and css, are all loaded from the database dynamically. Somewhat similar to how gulp works. In gulp, you don't have to worry too much about importing javascript and css manually in the main index.html. Gulp will take care of this for you, however, it can't help with that much else, especially if you're not planning on eventually hosting your AngularJS apps on Rails, Django or Flask.

Djangular takes the primary weakness and concern with Angular 1 and turns it into a super power. The weakness of AngularJS is it's complexity and difficulty to use and scale. I have heard made horror stories from startups where the Angular application would reach a critical point where newly added code would begin to break pre-existing code, this is a very common mistake, and it can turn your dream app into a nightmare. You'll most likely be faced with a big decision, abandon the app and startover? Or, try to fix it, and hope that it will not take several months. Meanwhile during this time, your users or customers are demanding more features and bug fixes at the same time. This is what turns away even the best and brightest developers from using AngularJS. And so, jQuery marches on as the most risk-free choice, but the drawback is an app that's stuck in the last decade.

This major drawback of AngularJS does have a solution to it, the development of AngularJS apps using Tom Papa's Style Guide . This guide will allow you to build massive, complex and scalable AngularJS single pages applications properly and with stability. This guide is the backbone of Djangular, and the reason why I was able to build Djangular by accident.
